1. Back up Contacts from iPhone 6#

Backing up the contacts from an iPhone 6 can be simple if you know how to go about it. To ensure a safe backup and access to your contacts on any other device, these 7 quick and easy steps will guide you through the process of transferring contacts from your iPhone 6 to an Android phone.

Make sure that your contacts are all synced to iCloud by tapping on iCloud Backup on the next page and by enabling the switch.

On the same page, select the Backup Now option. And then, on your Android phone, tap the Agree & Continue button on the Move to iOS app, and then tap Continue on the next few screens. Your iPhone will display a code on the screen while the Android phone will display the Find Your Code screen.

Once you enter the code from your iPhone, the Android phone will start the transfer. Once the transfer is complete, you’ll receive a notice on both phones. There, you can see all of the contacts that have been transferred to the Android phone from the iPhone 6. Finally, tap the Done button on both phones to complete the backup process.

2. Transferring Contacts to Android Device#

If you recently switched to an Android phone from an iPhone 6, you might be worried about how to transfer all of your contacts from the iPhone 6 to your new phone. Fortunately, it is easy to backup and transfer your contacts from your iPhone 6 to your Android phone. Here’s how you can do it:

First, open the Contacts app on your iPhone 6. Make sure all of your contacts are up to date, as this will ensure the transfer is accurate. Then, open the Settings app and select “Mail, Contacts, Calendars”. Tap “Import SIM Contacts” if you have contacts saved on your SIM card.

Next, you’ll have to export your contacts from your iPhone 6. To do this, select “Contacts”, then “Export Contacts”. You will be presented with several options for exporting your contacts. Select “vCard”, which supports the most used contact formats. Save your contacts to your computer.

Once your contacts have been saved to your computer, you can transfer them to your Android phone. Plug in your Android phone to your computer using a USB cable. Copy the vCard file to your Android phone’s internal storage, then open the Contacts app. Select “Import/Export”, then “Import from storage”. Select the vCard file you saved to your Android phone’s internal storage, and your contacts will be imported.

That’s all you need to do to transfer your contacts from your iPhone 6 to an Android phone. With just a few simple steps, you can make sure all of your contacts are saved and synced across both of your devices.

It can often be tough to transfer contacts from one device to another, especially if the two devices are running on different operating systems.
