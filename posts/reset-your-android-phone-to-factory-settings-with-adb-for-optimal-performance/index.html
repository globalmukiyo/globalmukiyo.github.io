How to Factory Reset Android Phone Using ADB#

Using ADB or Android Debug Bridge to reset your Android phone to factory settings can be your last resort when everything else fails. It is a powerful debugging tool that is used to send commands from the computer to your Android device when it is connected via USB cable. This makes it possible to reset your device to its default settings and wipe out all saved data. To facilitate the process, here are the steps to follow to reset an Android phone using ADB:

First, you need to enable USB debugging on your device. To do this, go to Settings and then About Phone. Scroll down to build number and tap on it seven times. This will enable the Developer Options from which you can enable USB debugging.

Next, you need to install ADB on your computer. You can do this by downloading the Android SDK from its website and then running the included installer. Once ADB is installed, connect your Android device to your computer using a USB cable and then open a command prompt on your computer and type in the command adb devices.

When the prompt prompts you to allow connection, accept it and type the next command adb reboot recovery. This will reboot your device into recovery mode. Once you are in the recovery mode, select the wipe data/factory reset option and your device will be reset to its original factory settings.
