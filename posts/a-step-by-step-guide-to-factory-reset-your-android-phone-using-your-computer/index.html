II. Resetting Your Android Phone#

Most of us have smartphones these days and they store a lot of important data on our phones. To keep our data safe, it’s a good practice to factory reset our phone every now and then. If you own an Android phone and you want to factory reset it, you can easily do it using your PC. Here’s how you can factory reset your Android phone using a PC. First, make sure you’ve downloaded and installed the latest version of Android SDK tools on your computer. Then, enable USB Debugging on your Android phone. To do this, go to Settings > Developer options > USB Debugging and turn it on. Now, connect your Android phone to your computer using a USB cable. Next, open the Android SDK tools on your computer and run ‘adb devices’ command in the command prompt window. This will display the connected device. Now, type in ‘fastboot devices’ and press Enter. This will display the list of connected devices in fastboot mode. Finally, type ‘fastboot oem factory reset’ and press Enter to factory reset your Android phone. Once the reset is complete, your phone will be reset to its original factory settings. Now, you can disconnect your phone from your computer and you’re all set.
