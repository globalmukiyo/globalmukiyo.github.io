To take a full-screen screenshot on your Samsung Chromebook, press the Ctrl + Show Windows keys at the same time. This will capture the entire screen, including any windows or menus that are open. If you prefer to capture a specific window or part of the screen, press Ctrl + Shift + Show Windows and the cursor will change into a crosshair. You can then click and drag the crosshair over the area that you would like to capture.

The screenshot will be saved automatically in the Downloads folder on your Chromebook. It will be in a .png format, which is ideal for sharing or posting online. You can also copy and paste the screenshot directly into an email or document.

Finally, you can also edit and annotate the screenshot before you share it. To do this, open the screenshot in the Chrome browser using the Chrome Canary app. Then you can use the annotation tool to mark up, highlight, or add arrows and text to your screenshot.

To learn how to take a screenshot on a Samsung Chromebook using the keyboard, read on.

First, press the Ctrl and Switch window keys at the same time. This will capture the entire screen in a screenshot. To capture just a portion of the screen, press the Ctrl and the Shift keys, followed by the Switch window key. You will then be able to select the area you want to capture.

The next step is to decide where you want to save the screenshot. By default, all screenshots taken on a Chromebook are saved in the downloads folder. However, you can easily change this in the settings. To do so, click the Settings icon in the lower-right corner of the screen and navigate to the Storage section. Then, select Change to choose a different folder to save your screenshots.

Finally, press the Ctrl, Shift and Switch window keys again to save the screenshot. The screenshot will instantly appear in the folder you selected. With just a few steps, you can easily capture what you need on a Samsung Chromebook.
