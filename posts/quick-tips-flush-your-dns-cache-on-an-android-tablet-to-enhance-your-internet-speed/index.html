Clearing the cache could help you access the websites you visited, and faster. If you have an Android tablet, here’s a quick step-by-step guide on how to flush your DNS cache.

First, install the DNS Scavenger app available from Google Play Store. Once installed, open the app, and click on the ‘Flush DNS Cache’ button. You’ll get a notification that the process is complete. Then, restart your Android tablet and you’re done. Your DNS cache is now cleared.

Bear in mind that this only works if your tablet is rooted and you need to set the app to be a system app. You will also need to configure the app’s settings to make it work correctly.

It’s important to note that when you flush your DNS cache on your Android tablet, your device will no longer be able to resolve or access websites using addresses saved in the DNS cache. If you visit a website and the website’s IP address has changed, your device will not be able to connect to the website until the DNS information has been updated.

So, if you’re experiencing slow internet speeds on your Android tablet, one of the first steps you should take is to flush your DNS cache. Installing the DNS Scavenger app is an easy-to-follow solution and will help you to access websites faster.

First, open the settings of your Android tablet. Navigate to the Wi-Fi tab, tap and hold on the network you are currently connected to, and then select Modify Network.

Next, go to Advanced Options and uncheck the ‘IPv6’ box if it is checked. Finally, select the option to flush the DNS Cache. Upon completion, restart the device and check if the issue is resolved.

Flushing the DNS Cache on an Android tablet can be easily done within minutes through the steps mentioned above. If the issue persists, it is recommended to restart your router and try again. Once finished, you will be able to access websites on your device normally.
