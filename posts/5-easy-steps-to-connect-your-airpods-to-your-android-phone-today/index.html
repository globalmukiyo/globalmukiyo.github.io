1. Check The Supported Devices#

Before attempting to connect your AirPods to your Android device, make sure it is one of the supported devices. AirPods are compatible with most Android devices using Bluetooth 5.0 or higher operating in Android 6.0 or higher. You can find the full list of compatible devices on the Apple Support website. Once you’ve verified your device is supported, you can get started with the connection process.

Next, make sure your AirPods are powered on and in pairing mode. Put your AirPods into the charging case and open the lid. Press and hold the setup button located on the back of the case until you see the status light flashing white. Your AirPods are now ready to pair.

Third, open the Bluetooth setting on your Android device. Make sure Bluetooth is enabled and then search for AirPods under the available devices. Select your AirPods from the list to begin the pairing process.

Fourth, the pairing process should be instantaneous. After your AirPods and Android device are connected, you’ll see the AirPods listed as an audio device under the Bluetooth settings. That’s it! You can now enjoy your AirPods with your Android device.

Finally, if you ever need to disconnect your AirPods from your Android device, simply go to the Bluetooth settings and select the ”forget” option. Or, you can press and hold the setup button on the back of the charging case for a few seconds until the status light begins flashing white and blue. This will disconnect your AirPods from your Android device.
