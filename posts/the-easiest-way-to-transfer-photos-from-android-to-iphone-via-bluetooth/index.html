- How to transfer photos from Android to iPhone via Bluetooth#

Transferring photos from Android to iPhone can usually be a pain. Fortunately, there is an easy way to do the job — using bluetooth! This guide will show you how to quickly and easily transfer photos from your Android phone to your iPhone.

First, turn on Bluetooth on both your Android and iPhone devices. Then, pair the two devices by selecting the name of the other device from the list available on your phones. Once they are paired, you will need to confirm the connection on both devices.

After your devices are connected, open your Android’s photo gallery and select the photos you want to transfer. Then, tap the ‘Share’ button and select ‘Bluetooth’ from the options. Choose your iPhone from the list of available devices, and the photos will be transferred to your iPhone.

Finally, open the Photos app on your iPhone and check your newly transferred images. That’s all there is to it! With just a few simple steps, you can now easily transfer photos from your Android to your iPhone via Bluetooth.

2. Enabling Bluetooth Connection#

Transferring photos from an Android device to an iPhone, while not a difficult procedure, can be a time consuming process. To speed up the process of transferring photos from an Android to an iPhone, Bluetooth can be used. To begin with, both the Android and the iPhone must have Bluetooth enabled. On the Android device, go to Settings and turn on Bluetooth. For the iPhone, swipe up from the bottom of the screen to open the control centre and then tap the Bluetooth icon to enable it.

Once Bluetooth is enabled, on the Android device, access the photos you want to transfer and tap the share button. Then choose Bluetooth from the list of options provided. On the iPhone, tap the Accept option when prompted. The photos will begin to transfer from the Android device to the iPhone.

To transfer photos in the opposite direction (iPhone to Android) the above steps need to be reversed. On the iPhone, open the photos you want to transfer and tap the share button. From the options list, choose Bluetooth. On the Android device, select the Accept option when prompted. The photos will begin to transfer from the iPhone to the Android device.

Once the transfer is complete a confirmation prompt will appear in both devices. To close the prompt, tap the OK/Done button. It is important to note that both Bluetooth devices must be close to each other for a successful transfer. That’s how to transfer photos from an Android to an iPhone via Bluetooth.

On iOS, open the Settings app and tap ‘Bluetooth’. Enable Bluetooth if it isn’t already and then select the device to pair with from the list. Depending on the device, you may have to enter a PIN code after the connection is established.

Once your devices are paired, you should now be able to transfer files between them. Open the File Manager app on your Android device and select the photos you want to transfer.
