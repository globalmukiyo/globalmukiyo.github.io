1. Preparation for Sharing Windows Screen on Android TV#

Technology has made it easier to share our work with others. When it comes to sharing our computer screen, there are numerous ways to go about doing it. One of the easy methods is by using an Android TV and a Windows device. Here are the steps to share Windows screen to Android TV.

First, connect both the Windows device and Android TV to the same Wi-Fi network. On the Windows device, open the Action Center and select the Connect option. Then, select the Android TV name from the list of available devices. Accept permission to connect to the Android TV on both devices.

Second, open the Settings app on the Android TV and navigate to Network & Internet. Then, select the Wi-Fi tab and click on Wi-Fi Details. You will be provided with a randomized IP address and PC name. This should be noted for future use.

Third, turn on the Project feature on the Windows device. Type Project in the taskbar search box and click on project to this PC. Select the ‘available everywhere’ and ‘first time only’ options. Enter the randomized IP address and PC name from the Android TV.

Finally, return to the Android TV and click on the notification you received. It should be something like ‘screen shared’. Click on accept to allow the connection and you are now able to see the contents of the Windows PC on the Android TV.

2. Sharing Windows Screen on Android TV#

Sharing your Windows screen directly to Android TV is much easier than most people think. To get started, all you need is the right devices, a few cables, and some preparation. Ensuring the devices are compatible, setting up the cables, and downloading the right app ahead of time are the three key steps to get your screens to mirror each other.

First, check to make sure the device you’re using is compatible with your TV. Many new Windows and Android TVs have a built-in feature that allows you to easily connect them. Then, get the right cables. Most Android TVs use micro-HDMI cables for mirroring, while some Windows use an HDMI to VGA adapter. Some research on the type of cable you need may be required.

Once the cables are in place, you’ll need to download the right app. For many Android TVs, the official Android TV app allows you to mirror your Windows screen to the TV. Depending on the type of device you’re using, some alternative apps may be worth exploring.

Finally, make sure your devices are near each other. To successfully mirror your Windows screen on your Android TV, you’ll need to be in close proximity of the devices. After preparing the devices, cables, and apps, you should be all set to begin mirroring your Windows screen.

First, make sure that your PC and Android TV are connected to the same wireless network. Then, go to your PC’s network settings and select To share, or access files and devices on this network. Next, click the Add a device button and enter the same PIN for both devices.

Now, open the Android TV app on your PC. From the App menu, select the Shared screen option. Then, press the Connect button and select your Android TV from the list of available devices.

Once the connection is established, you’ll be able to see the Windows PC screen on your Android TV. To start sharing audio and video, simply drag and drop the files you want to share into the shared screen window. You can also use the wireless mouse or keyboard to control your Windows PC.
